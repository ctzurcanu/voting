Decision-making

The right to make decisions is not natural. It is earned by proving control of entropy. Therefore all life has earned the right to decide something.

As a form of life, an entity has the right to decide about the resources harvested, and about the continuation of its kind as much as it does not impede the same to others.

The virtue of decision-making is recursive: it increases as it allows for more complex decisions to be based on previous decisions.

In the following we will treat the cases where the distinction between what is the right of the individual and what is the right of the community gets unclear.

It is easy to decide as an individual, for yourself; however, in the context of a larger group, a community, the need for respecting protocols appears.

There are these classes of protocols for making decisions:

  1. Voting
  2. Delegation of Voting
  3. Dictate
  4. Necessity
  5. Mathematical Proof

Voting​

Voting as a decision-making process has these advantages:

Advantages​

  • leaves most freedom to the involved parties
  • it is non-violent
  • involves the most people
  • allows for individual virtue to become a community virtue
  • allows for the individual to exercise most virtues
  • it is the most complex
  • the most composable
  • the most flexible
  • the most stable

Disadvantages:​

  • leaves a lot of freedom to the involved parties
  • allows for individual vices to become community-wide vices
  • it is slow
  • hard to scale
  • hard to verify
  • allows for the individual to exercise most vices

Delegation of Voting​

Has all the advantages and disadvantages of voting.

Additional Advantages​

  • easier to scale

Additional Disadvantages​

  • decreases individual responsibility
  • depending on the degree, it may become a dictate

Dictate​

Advantages​

  • fast
  • easy to verify
  • smallest responsable party

Disadvantages​

  • does not encourage individual evolution
  • it is fully dependent on a few or even one individual
  • unstable

Necessity​

A dictate by causes external to the will of the citizens:

  • by natural causes
  • by foreign dictat

Mathematical Proof​

A dictate by unchanging causes that are fully describable.

Advantages​

  • verifiable by design
  • automateable
  • maximum scalability
  • maximum speed
  • optimized for virtue
  • stable

Disadvantages​

  • if mistakes exist in proving, they are greater, systemic

From the point of view of increasing virtue:​

The virtue of Mathematical Proof is recursive: it increases by the amount of other proofs that use it in their construction. It has an essence similar to decision-making.

We conclude that the ideal basis for making decisions is Mathematical Proof.

However, because such proofs are rare or hard to manufacture, the community should settle for the second-best: Voting.
